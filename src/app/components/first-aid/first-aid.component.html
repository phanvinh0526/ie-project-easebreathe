How do you tell if your child is having an asthma attack?

Asthma attack symptoms can vary from episode to episode in the same child, and so it is important for parents to observe their child for signs and symptoms to be able to help their child, in case of asthma attack.

The most common and evident signs of an asthma attack include:

  • Wheezing
  • Shortness of breath
  • Chest Tightness
  • Persistent coughing

Additionally, the signs and symptoms mentioned below are what are seen in severe asthma attack cases. If you see these symptoms in your child, you should immediately call for an ambulance [dial 000], as this is an asthma emergency.

  • Gasping for breath with no wheezing due to lack of air exchange.
  • Severe chest tightness
  • Being able to speak little or none due to breathing difficulty
  • Color of lips changes to blue
  • Need of using reliever medication every 2-3 hours
  • Sucking-in of throat and rib muscles

What can I do to help my child having an asthma attack?

Asthma can worsen quickly over a short period of time and so it is crucial for parents to look for the signs and symptoms mentioned above to decide severity of the attack.

If your child starts experiencing asthma symptoms, it is best to start asthma first aid instead of waiting for the attack to get severe.
