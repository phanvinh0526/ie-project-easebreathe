Physical Activities Recommendation

Good Control Asthma

If your child has good or well controlled asthma, he/she has two symptoms per week or less. These symptoms do not limit normal activity of the child and will rarely wake him/her up at night. The use of relievers, such as short-acting beta-agonists (SABA), is two times per week or less. Also, the lung functioning is normal or near-normal.

Swimming

Swimming is a healthy form of aerobic exercise that children with asthma can participate in. The air you breathe while swimming is warm and moist preventing any kind of airway irritation. Swimming also helps improve lung functionality and increases cardiopulmonary fitness. In fitness level will prevent trigger of asthmatic.

Walking

Walking is moderate type of physical activity, which strengths lung functionality and assists in preventing the resistance in breathing experienced by asthmatics. It is important to consider the pace of walking.The weather conditions need to be taken into consideration as well before plannign to go.

Jogging

Jogging have a positive impact on lung muscles and cardiovascular fitness. Participating in jogging/running will help increase maximal breathing reserve which can improve asthmatic condition over time. Since, jogging can be quite strenuous for a child with asthma.

Yoga

Yoga training can not only help improve your child°Øs asthmatic symptoms but also help relax their body and mind. Yogic breathing, also known as pranayama, teaches children to use their rib cage, chest, diaphragm, throat, and nasal cavity to bring oxygen into the body and to release carbon dioxide.

Volleyball

Volleyball can be a physical activity that your child can participate in, since the movement required to play this game does not require the player to run around much compared to sports like soccer. You need to ensure that your child is trained with a controlled pace to build the fitness level step-by-step.

Softball

Softball similar to the game of baseball allows players to take enough time for rest and drink water to make sure the player is hydrated. You need to follow general precautions mentioned in the table. If you notice trigger of any symptom in your child, see to it that your child takes break from the game till he/she is relieved from the symptoms.

Tennis

Tennis as a sport will allow your child to expend energy on the court with regular breaks in between the games with access to water (prevent asthmatic symptom due to dehydration.) and medication (if need be). It also enables the player to control the pace of the game and if it’s a doubles match.

Cycling

Cycling at a leisurely pace should not trigger symptoms of asthma. It is important to consider the pace of cycling and the steepness of the ground you are cycling on. Mountain biking is definitely not advisable since rapid process of breathing can dry out airways causing asthmatic symptoms.
