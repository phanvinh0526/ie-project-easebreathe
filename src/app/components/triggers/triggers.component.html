Weather

Some individuals with asthma can suffer with severe symptoms during certain weather conditions than otherwise. Cold, dry air can induce asthma symptoms for asthmatics who participate in physical activities. Extreme hot, humid and windy weather causes growth of mold and spread of pollen through air, which can trigger asthmatic symptoms.

Asthma

Triggers

A trigger is something that sets off or starts asthma symptoms. Everyone’s asthma is different, and everyone has different triggers. For most people with asthma, triggers are only a problem when their asthma is not well-controlled with medicine. The three main triggers of asthmatic symptoms in children are pollens, weather and allergens.

Pollen

Pollen is powder like substance produced by trees, plants and weeds. ‘Thunderstorm asthma’ is a potent mix of pollens, weather conditions and rain that can trigger severe asthma symptoms. When rain droplets crash into airborne pollen, the pollen grains are broken into tiny particles. These particles can then get further and deeper inside your lungs than the larger pollen grains, and so trigger a worse asthma response.

Allergens

Allergens are any substance that can induce allergic reactions to substances in the environment that are harmless to other people. Asthma symptoms can occur when an allergen is inhaled into lungs of a sensitive person. This leads to swelling of the lining of the airways and tightening of the muscles around the airway, which causes narrowing of the airway and difficulty in breathing. Some of the allergens leading to Asthmatic symptoms are house dust mites, pets (animal dander), pollen and mold.
